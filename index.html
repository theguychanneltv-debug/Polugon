What Is a Polygon?

A polygon is a flat, closed shape made only from straight line segments. The sides connect end-to-end and form corners called vertices.

Simple rule: If a shape is closed and has only straight sides, it is a polygon.

Parts of a Polygon

  • Sides: The straight line segments that make the polygon.
  • Vertices: The corner points where two sides meet.
  • Angles: The spaces formed inside the polygon at each vertex.
  • Interior: The inside area of the polygon.

How to Make or Draw a Polygon

To make a polygon, you need straight sides that connect all the way around. You can draw one on paper with a pencil and ruler, or you can make one digitally using lines or shapes.

1

Choose the number of sides

Decide how many sides your polygon will have. For example, 3 sides makes a triangle and 6 sides makes a hexagon.

2

Draw straight lines

Use a ruler to draw each side. Polygons cannot have curved sides.

3

Connect the endpoints

Every line should meet another line at the corners. The shape must be closed, with no gaps.

4

Check the shape

Count the sides, vertices, and angles. They should all match the polygon name.

Tip for regular polygons: If you want all sides and angles to be equal, draw points evenly around a circle and connect neighboring points with straight lines.
Interior angle sum: For a polygon with n sides, the total inside angles add up to (n − 2) × 180°. Example: a pentagon has (5 − 2) × 180° = 540°.

Regular vs. Irregular Polygons

A regular polygon has all sides equal and all angles equal. An irregular polygon does not have all sides and angles equal.

Example: A square is a regular polygon because all four sides and angles are equal.

What Is Not a Polygon?

  • A circle is not a polygon because it has a curved edge.
  • An open shape is not a polygon because the sides do not fully connect.
  • A shape with curved sides is not a polygon.
Fun Fact: The word "polygon" comes from Greek words meaning "many angles."
